Tinner's Solution

In modern life, "What should we eat today?" has become a daily obsession. Tinner solves this by simplifying the decision-making process to the maximum.

  • Single-View Interface

    Only displays one dish at a time. Users are not distracted by other options, minimizing information overload.

  • Gamified Experience

    "Swipe Left (Pass) - Swipe Right (Like)". This turns stressful food selection into a fun and engaging game.

  • Decision Maker Tool

    The "Wheel of Destiny" feature randomly selects one restaurant from your liked list, helping to "close the deal" instantly.

  • Visual-First

    Build an interface focused purely on food imagery to stimulate the appetite ("Eat with your eyes").

Project Purpose

Designed to completely remove the friction of the Choice Matrix and the Paradox of Choice in food discovery.

THE PAIN POINT

The Paradox of Choice.

Current food delivery apps display lists of hundreds of restaurants. On average, users spend 15-20 minutes just scrolling and debating, often leading to choosing a random unsatisfactory dish or skipping the meal entirely.

  • Choice Matrix (Information Overload).

  • Time-Consuming daily obsession.

CORE MVP

From opening to deciding in under 5 minutes.

Focusing on 2 core features that fully resolve the main User Flow, delivering a seamless, "Visual-first" interface that is intuitive and easy to use for non-tech-savvy users.

  • Swipe-to-Match Logic reveals restaurants instantly.

  • Visual Restaurant Info Cards for the final decision.
